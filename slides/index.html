Ahmed bn tolon mosque

Architectural Features: Constructed primarily of red brick, a distinctive feature that sets it apart from many other mosques in Egypt, which typically use stone. Features a square floor plan with a large central courtyard surrounded by four arcades (covered walkways supported by columns or arches). The minarets (towers) are particularly notable. The main minaret is a spiral minaret, modeled after those in Samarra, Iraq, with a unique helical staircase winding around the outside.
